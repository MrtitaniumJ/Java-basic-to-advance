## Interview Process

### Timeline
- **Application to First Round**: 1-2 weeks
- **First Round to Onsite**: 3-4 weeks
- **Onsite to Decision**: 1-2 weeks
- **Total Duration**: 5-8 weeks

### Interview Structure

#### Round 1: Phone Screening (20-30 minutes)
**Conducted by**: Recruiter
**Content**:
- Background and experience discussion
- Interest in Meta and specific role
- General technical questions (not usually coding)
- Salary expectations
**Pass Rate**: ~60-70%

#### Round 2: Technical Phone Interview (45-60 minutes)
**Conducted by**: Software engineer
**Content**:
- 1-2 medium coding problems
- Discussion of approach and optimization
- Problem-solving methodology
**Tool**: Shared coding platform
**Pass Rate**: ~30-40%

#### Round 3: Onsite - Technical Interview 1 (45-60 minutes)
**Conducted by**: Senior engineer (frontend, backend, or full-stack)
**Content**:
- 1 medium to hard coding problem
- Whiteboarding or online coding
- Optimization and follow-up questions
- Real-world application discussion
**Focus**: Problem-solving, code quality, optimization
**Pass Rate**: ~30-40%

#### Round 4: Onsite - Technical Interview 2 (45-60 minutes)
**Conducted by**: Different senior engineer
**Content**:
- 1-2 medium to hard coding problems
- May include systems design elements
- Topic could be different from Round 3
**Focus**: Breadth of knowledge, technical depth
**Pass Rate**: ~30-40%

#### Round 5: Onsite - System Design (45-60 minutes, mid-level+)
**Conducted by**: Tech lead or senior engineer
**Content**:
- Design a system at Meta scale (billions of users)
- Discussion of architecture, databases, caching, messaging
- Trade-offs between consistency, availability, cost
- Real-world Meta examples
**Examples**:
- Design Facebook News Feed
- Design Instagram Recommendations
- Design WhatsApp infrastructure
- Design Messenger for billions of users
**Pass Rate**: ~40-50%

#### Round 6: Behavioral/Culture Fit (20-30 minutes)
**Conducted by**: Manager, HR, or senior engineer
**Content**:
- Why Meta? Understanding of their mission
- Teamwork and collaboration examples
- Handling ambiguity and rapid change
- Failure and learning experiences
- Questions about career aspirations
**Key Themes**: Adaptability, collaboration, ambition, learning
**Pass Rate**: ~50-60%

#### Optional Round 7: Debrief/Manager Round
**Conducted by**: Hiring manager
**Content**: Team and role specifics, final discussions
**Note**: More conversational and confirmatory

### Onsite Format
- Usually 1 day or sometimes split across 2 days
- 4-5 rounds back-to-back
- Lunch with team (informal, not evaluated)
- Office tour

### Overall Pass Rate
- **First Round**: 60-70% pass
- **Second Round**: 30-40% pass
- **Final Offer Rate**: 5-10% for all applicants

## Tips and Tricks

### Coding Interview Tips

1. **Optimize for Speed AND Quality**: Meta values both
   - Write clean, readable code quickly
   - Handle edge cases systematically
   - Optimize time/space complexity
   - Test thoroughly

2. **Think About Real-World Application**: Meta uses real scenarios
   - Discuss how your solution scales
   - Consider production concerns
   - Think about real-world constraints
   - Mention caching, databases when relevant

3. **Ask Clarifying Questions**: Essential to understand problem
   - Input/output constraints
   - Edge cases and special scenarios
   - Can you use specific data structures?
   - What's the scale of data?

4. **Code Quality Matters**: Meta engineers are meticulous
   - Clear variable and function names
   - Proper error handling
   - Comments for complex logic
   - Comprehensive test cases

5. **Discuss Trade-offs**: Show engineering maturity
   - Time vs space complexity
   - Simplicity vs optimization
   - Readability vs performance

### System Design Tips

1. **Start with Scope**: Understand what you're building
   - Daily active users
   - Read/write ratio
   - Consistency requirements
   - Latency and throughput needs

2. **Think About Scale from the Start**: Meta scale is unique
   - Billions of users
   - Real-time interactions
   - Global distribution
   - Petabytes of data

3. **Discuss Caching**: Critical for Meta systems
   - Multi-level caching (CDN, memory, storage)
   - Cache invalidation strategies
   - Handling cache misses

4. **Address Recommendation Algorithms**: Meta specialty
   - Ranking and personalization
   - A/B testing frameworks
   - Feature engineering

5. **Real-Time Considerations**: Essential for Meta products
   - WebSocket connections
   - Message queues
   - Stream processing
   - Notification systems

### Behavioral Interview Tips

1. **Show Genuine Interest in Meta**: Know their mission
   - Understand their products deeply
   - Know their vision (connecting people, metaverse)
   - Reference specific initiatives
   - Show enthusiasm for their problems

2. **Demonstrate Adaptability**: Meta values this
   - Stories of adapting to change
   - Learning new technologies
   - Pivoting strategies
   - Working in ambiguous situations

3. **Emphasize Impact**: Meta cares about results
   - Quantifiable achievements
   - Products that helped users
   - Scale of your impact
   - Continuous improvement

4. **Prepare for Culture Questions**: Be authentic
   - How you handle feedback
   - Work-life balance approach
   - Team collaboration style
   - Learning mindset examples

5. **Ask Good Questions**: Show curiosity
   - About the team's current challenges
   - Vision for the product
   - Culture and values in practice
   - Career growth opportunities

## Previous Interview Patterns

**Junior Engineer (0-2 years)**
- Rounds: Phone screening + phone tech + onsite (4-5 rounds)
- Difficulty: Easy to medium coding problems
- Duration: 5-7 weeks
- Focus: Fundamentals, learning ability, passion
- Outcome: Quick decision post-onsite

**Mid-Level Engineer (2-4 years)**
- Rounds: Phone tech + onsite (4-5 rounds including system design)
- Difficulty: Medium to hard coding problems
- Duration: 4-6 weeks
- Focus: Problem-solving, system design, technical depth
- Outcome: 1-2 week decision timeline

**Senior Engineer (5+ years)**
- Rounds: Phone tech + onsite (4-6 rounds)
- Difficulty: Hard problems, complex system design
- Duration: 4-8 weeks
- Focus: Architecture, leadership, impact
- Outcome: 1-3 week decision timeline

### Common Mistakes

- Not asking clarifying questions
- Overcomplicating solutions
- Poor communication about approach
- Missing system design importance
- Generic behavioral answers
- Not showing knowledge of Meta's products/culture
- Weak handling of edge cases
- Not discussing real-world concerns
